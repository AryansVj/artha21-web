What is a Photo-Story?

“To me, photography is an art of observation. It’s about finding something interesting in an ordinary place… I’ve found it has little to do with the things you see and everything to do with the way you see them.”— Elliott Erwitt

© Elliott Erwitt | Nils Jorgensen | duane michals | Ashley Manley | Elliott Erwitt | Adde Adesokan


Can you observe something beyond what is normally seen? Can capture a story?


A Photo-Story is a photograph or a sequence of photographs that can tell a story. The story can be a simple incident or a deep idea revealed by the way you capture. The story can be as simple and funny as the dog and the man sequence or as deep as the two gloves which are photographs from the same artist. Put yourself as the narrator and look for for frames that assemble a single concept or an incident. It does not has to be shots taken in sequence as long as it assembles the single story or idea you want to express

The submissions should be made as a sequence of 2 - 6 photographs. You are free to explore and create a story. What is evaluated in the contest is the story behind the photographs and the effort you have taken to capture it. So be creative and keep experimenting.
